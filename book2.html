Francis Itty Cora

By T. D. Ramakrishnan



Francis Itty Cora Malayalam historical novel written by T. D. Ramakrishnan. It blends fiction, history, and speculative storytelling and is widely appreciated for its imaginative narrative and controversial themes.

The 2009 novel received acclaim from critics for its incorporation of global historical characters and knowledge available.[2][3] Like The Journeyer, the novel deals with the exploration of a merchant named Francis Itty Cora, hailing from the Kerala of 15th century. Among the historical characters include Vasco da Gama, Hypatia and Lorenzo de' Medici. The current global knowledge include Abu Ghraib torture and prisoner abuse and primacy of Kerala school of mathematics. Like The Da Vinci Code, this novel is of Christian background and tries to convince the reader an alternative possibility of what happened in the past in the Nazrani community of Malabar.
